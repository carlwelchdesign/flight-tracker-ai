# Risk Register

Scores use likelihood and impact from 1 (low) to 5 (high). Owners are roles until named people are assigned.

| ID | Risk | L | I | Trigger | Mitigation | Owner | Review gate |
| --- | --- | ---: | ---: | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| R-01 | Flight-data license does not permit intended commercial situational-display use | 4 | 5 | Self-service or proposed contract excludes B2B display, SaaS processing, retention, redistribution, source combination, or upstream data | Keep simulation through M1; require written enterprise terms and legal review using the FT-301 provider questionnaire before integration | Product/Legal | M0/M3 |
| R-02 | Stale or partial data appears current | 4 | 5 | Feed delay exceeds threshold without visible warning | Track event/receive/process times; show stale and degraded states; test outages | Backend | M2 |
| R-03 | Alert fatigue makes the product unusable | 4 | 4 | High dismissal rate or duplicate alerts | Dedupe keys, severity policy, suppression windows, dismissal reasons, replay tuning | Product | M2/M4 |
| R-04 | Users mistake advisory output for certified guidance | 3 | 5 | Copy or UI implies authority | Explicit advisory labeling, human review, legal review, role-based controls | Product/Trust | M4 |
| R-05 | Route–hazard geometry is wrong around time or altitude | 3 | 5 | False negative/positive in validation cases | Version geometry and rules; include altitude/time; golden fixtures; independent review | Backend | M2 |
| R-06 | Rust slows iteration due to unfamiliarity | 3 | 3 | Repeated implementation delays | Modular monolith, documented patterns, narrow dependencies, strong fixtures | Engineering | M1 |
| R-07 | External feed outages break demos and tests | 4 | 3 | Provider unavailable | Deterministic replay, cached fixtures, circuit breakers, degraded UI | Backend | M1/M2 |
| R-08 | Tenant data leaks across operators | 2 | 5 | Query lacks tenant scope | Tenant key in schema, scoped repository APIs, authorization tests | Security | M3 |
| R-09 | LLM summary omits or fabricates operational detail | 3 | 5 | Draft differs materially from evidence | Constrained structured inputs, citations, human approval, evaluation set | AI/Product | M5 |
| R-10 | Optimization cannot be validated with available truth data | 4 | 4 | No historical baseline or outcome data | Make data availability a research gate before product commitment | Product/Data | M5 |
| R-11 | Government feed is used beyond its permitted or supported operational role | 3 | 5 | SCDS, NMS, or Internet weather data is treated as a sole operational source or as having an SLA | Confirm service-specific permitted use; keep advisory labeling, redundant/degraded behavior, source provenance, and explicit access gates | Product/Legal/Backend | M2/M3 |
| R-12 | Restricted or privacy-limited aircraft data is displayed or retained incorrectly | 3 | 5 | LADD/PIA/blocked tail appears without authorization or persists beyond allowed retention | Capture provider and FAA obligations in contracts; model visibility entitlement and retention policy; add blocked-tail and deletion tests | Security/Legal | M3 |
| R-13 | Provider data is processed by an AI system outside the licensed scope | 3 | 5 | Provider records, normalized facts, evidence, screenshots, or outputs reach an LLM/ML service without clause-level authorization | Keep provider data out of AI inputs by default; require prior written authorization defining fields, purposes, vendors, retention, logging, training, and deletion; enforce the approved policy at the AI boundary | Legal/Security/AI | M3/M5 |
| R-14 | Compromised runtime credentials cannot be rotated or revoked safely | 3 | 5 | Shared assertion, identity, database, or provider credential is exposed or suspected without a rehearsed cutover | Managed environment-separated secrets, normal/emergency rotation, short-lived assertions, revocation drill, and no-secret browser/build/log verification | Security/Platform | M4 |
| R-15 | Operational or licensed data survives its approved retention or deletion boundary | 3 | 5 | Expired, revoked, terminated, or restricted records remain in primary storage, exports, logs, or restored backups | Field/source-aware retention, deletion inventory, preview/approval, tombstones, deletion audit, and restore verification | Legal/Security/Backend | M3/M4 |
| R-16 | Malformed or adversarial provider traffic poisons state or exhausts ingestion | 3 | 5 | Oversized, invalid, duplicate, out-of-order, or high-rate messages cause backlog, worker failure, or misleading facts | Bounded adapters, schema/size/rate limits, quarantine, idempotency, degradation signals, adversarial fixtures, and FT-402 drills | Backend/Security | M3/M4 |
| R-17 | Users treat an advisory or simulated surface as authoritative live guidance | 2 | 5 | Persistent environment/advisory label is missing or inconsistent during queue, empty, loading, degraded, or error states | Legal/Product-approved wording, persistent environment/source-authority label, human review, and full-state UI tests | Product/Legal/Web | M4 |
| R-18 | A free feed's terms change or the implementation violates its attribution, caching, or redistribution boundary | 3 | 4 | ADSB.lol changes its ODbL/API policy; Airplanes.live changes its non-commercial API policy or rate limit; either requires a key; or the app persists, caches, exports, redistributes, or fails to attribute provider data | Revalidate both providers before public launch and periodically thereafter; enforce an ephemeral `no-store` Rust adapter, provider-specific linked attribution, no persistence/export/LLM use, bounded polling, a shared one-request-per-second Airplanes.live limiter, and deterministic replay fallback; require a new rights review before commercial use | Product/Backend | M3/M4 |
| R-19 | Public attention explanations imply that live aircraft have route, destination, or alert evidence the source did not provide | 3 | 5 | A live position receives a replay-derived route, inferred destination, unsupported score, or authoritative wording | Evaluate only evidence-backed replay cases publicly; expose not-evaluated and partial-evidence states; separate observations, deterministic rules, and estimates; verify copy in FT-413 | Product/Trust/Backend | M4.1 |
| R-20 | Shareable URLs leak protected state or become misleading after ephemeral live data expires | 3 | 4 | A URL contains tenant or alert identifiers, raw fields, or reopens a departed aircraft as though it were retained | Allowlist public URL fields; bound and normalize parameters; preserve region/view while showing an explicit aircraft-no-longer-present state; add authorization and stale-link tests | Web/Security | M4.1 |
| R-21 | TAF or PIREP context is presented as a complete or aircraft-specific current condition | 3 | 5 | Forecast periods, old pilot reports, missing locations, or altitude-limited reports are attached to an aircraft without supporting evidence | Show source, issue/report/valid times, distance, altitude, freshness and coverage limits; keep airport/nearby-report selection separate from aircraft facts; test partial and stale cases | Weather/Product/Trust | M4.1 |
